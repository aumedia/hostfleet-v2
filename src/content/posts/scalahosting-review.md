ScalaHosting occupies a different niche than the discount shared hosts we track. Its entry-level plan is a **managed VPS** starting at **$29.95/month** — far above the shared-host teaser prices from Hostinger, Bluehost, or SiteGround.

That price gap is the first thing most buyers notice. The more interesting part of the story is what happens later: ScalaHosting is one of only two providers in our current pricing file — alongside Cloudways — where the **intro price and renewal price are identical**. No teaser rate, no renewal shock.

## What ScalaHosting gets right

- **Flat pricing.** No intro bait, no renewal markup on the tracked plan.

- **Managed VPS positioning.** This is one of the cleaner step-up options if you want something above shared hosting.

- **Unlimited sites on the tracked entry plan.** That matters if you are consolidating several WordPress installs.

- **Better measured speed than SiteGround.** The current median gap is about **259.8 ms** in ScalaHosting’s favor.

## What holds it back

- **The price barrier is real.** $29.95/month is a hard sell for a first website.

- **The slower tail is still heavy.** 150 of 1041 checks crossed 1 second, and the p90 sits at 1227.6 ms.

- **It is not the speed leader.** Cloudways and Hostinger both look better on raw response metrics.

## Who should consider ScalaHosting?

- Buyers who care more about long-term pricing honesty than teaser intro rates

- Users moving beyond entry-level shared hosting and willing to pay for managed VPS positioning

- People hosting multiple sites who value flat pricing and unlimited-site limits

ScalaHosting makes less sense if you need the cheapest starting price or the best raw speed signal.

## Important caveats

- This is still an early monitoring window, not a multi-month production benchmark.

- We measure homepage TTFB, not full WordPress page-load speed or admin responsiveness.

- The tracked ScalaHosting plan is a managed VPS, so it is not an apples-to-apples comparison with budget shared hosting.

- We have not independently tested support quality, migrations, or uptime yet.

## Bottom line

**ScalaHosting is not the fastest host we track, and it is not the cheapest.** What it offers is flat pricing and a managed VPS starting point in a market full of teaser-rate games. If you are tired of intro-rate marketing and want to pay one number for the long haul, ScalaHosting is a credible option.

If price sensitivity is higher than renewal-risk sensitivity, [Hostinger](https://gethostfleet.com/hostinger-review/) remains the better first stop.
