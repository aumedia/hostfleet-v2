One of the most common hosting questions is not “what brand should I buy?” but “what kind of hosting do I actually need?” That is the right question to ask first. Picking the right hosting type usually matters more than obsessing over brand marketing on day one.

## Shared hosting

Shared hosting is the cheapest and simplest option. Your website shares a server with many others, which keeps costs low but can also make performance less predictable.

**Best for:** personal sites, blogs, simple business sites, and first projects.

**Current HostFleet examples:** [Hostinger](https://hostfleet.net/hostinger-review/), [Bluehost](https://hostfleet.net/bluehost-review/), and [SiteGround](https://hostfleet.net/siteground-review/) all sit in the shared-hosting lane, but they make very different pricing and performance trade-offs.

## Cloud hosting

Cloud hosting usually gives you better flexibility and easier scaling than entry-level shared hosting. It costs more, but it is often a better fit once traffic and business importance increase.

**Best for:** growing businesses, stores, and sites that may need to scale quickly.

**Current HostFleet example:** [Cloudways](https://hostfleet.net/cloudways-review/) is the clearest cloud-style option in our current dataset — faster than the shared hosts on median TTFB, but at a much higher monthly floor.

## VPS hosting

A VPS gives you dedicated resources inside a virtual server environment. That usually means more control, more consistency, and more responsibility.

**Best for:** advanced users, agencies, larger sites, and projects that need custom server setups.

**Current HostFleet example:** [ScalaHosting](https://hostfleet.net/scalahosting-review/) is the best current example of a managed-VPS-style offer in our tracked set: pricier upfront, but flatter on renewal pricing than the shared hosts.

## How to choose

- Choose **shared hosting** if you want the lowest cost and easiest setup.

- Choose **cloud hosting** if you need more flexibility and smoother scaling.

- Choose **VPS hosting** if you need dedicated resources or server-level control.
